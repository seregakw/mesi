ENGINE REMOVAL/INSTALLATION [ZJ, ZY, Z6]

Warning
•  Fuel vapor is hazardous. It can very easily ignite, causing serious injury and damage. Always keep sparks and flames away from fuel.
•  Fuel line spills and leakage are dangerous. Fuel can ignite and cause serious injuries or death and damage. Fuel can also irritate skin and eyes. To prevent this, always complete the “Fuel Line Safety Procedure”. (See BEFORE SERVICE PRECAUTION [ZJ, ZY, Z6].)
Note
•  Perform the engine and transaxle component removal/installation from below the vehicle.

1.  Remove the following parts:

(1)  Battery cover, battery box, battery clamp, battery and battery tray (See BATTERY REMOVAL/INSTALLATION [ZJ, ZY, Z6].)
(2)  Front wheels and tires (See GENERAL PROCEDURES (SUSPENSION).)
(3)  Under cover and splash shields
(4)  Flesh-air duct, air hose and air cleaner component (See INTAKE-AIR SYSTEM REMOVAL/INSTALLATION [ZJ, ZY, Z6].)
(5)  Accelerator cable and bracket (without throttle valve actuator)
(6)  Drive belt (See DRIVE BELT REPLACEMENT [ZJ, ZY, Z6].)

2.  Drain the following fluids:

(1)  ATF (ATX) or transaxle oil (MTX) (See AUTOMATIC TRANSAXLE FLUID (ATF) REPLACEMENT [FN4A-EL].) (See TRANSAXLE OIL REPLACEMENT [F35M-R].)
(2)  Engine coolant (See ENGINE COOLANT REPLACEMENT [ZJ, ZY, Z6].)
(3)  P/S fluid (with mechanical type) (See AIR BLEEDING.)

3.  Disconnect the purge solenoid ventilation hose.

4.  Disconnect the P/S return hose from the P/S oil pump side. (with mechanical type)

5.  Disconnect the P/S pressure pipe from the steering gear side. (with mechanical type)

6.  Disconnect the P/S return hose from the P/S reserve tank side. (with mechanical type)

7.  Remove the following parts:

(1)  Member (See EXHAUST SYSTEM REMOVAL/INSTALLATION [ZJ, ZY, Z6].)
(2)  Front crossmember, front stabilizer, lower arm, steering gear and No.1 engine mount rubber component (See STEERING GEAR AND LINKAGE REMOVAL/INSTALLATION.)
(3)  Drive shaft (See DRIVE SHAFT REMOVAL/INSTALLATION.)
(4)  P/S reserve tank with the hose still connected (with mechanical type)
(5)  Coolant reserve tank with the hose still connected
(6)  Cooling fan component (See RADIATOR REMOVAL/INSTALLATION [ZJ, ZY, Z6].)
(7)  ATF hose, selector cable and wiring harness (ATX) (See AUTOMATIC TRANSAXLE REMOVAL/INSTALLATION (ZY, Z6) [FN4A-EL].)
(8)  Select cable and shift cable (MTX) (See MANUAL TRANSAXLE REMOVAL/INSTALLATION [F35M-R].)
(9)  Clutch release cylinder with the pipe still connected (MTX) (See CLUTCH RELEASE CYLINDER REMOVAL/INSTALLATION [F35M-R, G35M-R, G66M-R, A26M-R].)
(10)  Earth (No.3 engine mount)
(11)  The A/C compressor with the pipes still connected.
Note
•  Secure the A/C compressor using wire or rope so that it is out of the way.

8.  Disconnect the following parts:

(1)  Brake vacuum hose
(2)  Fuel hose (See QUICK RELEASE CONNECTOR REMOVAL/INSTALLATION [ZJ, ZY, Z6].)
(3)  Heater hose
(4)  Upper and lower radiator hose
(5)  Main silencer (See EXHAUST SYSTEM REMOVAL/INSTALLATION [ZJ, ZY, Z6].)

9.  Remove in the order indicated in the table.

10.  Install in the reverse order of removal.

Caution
•  Do not tighten the No.1 engine mount rubber installation bolt before installing the No.3 engine mount.

11.  Inspect the following and adjust them if necessary.

•  Front wheel alignment (See FRONT WHEEL ALIGNMENT.)
•  Pulley and belt for runout and contact.
•  Leakage of engine oil, engine coolant, ATF, MT oil, and fuel.
•  Ignition timing and idle speed. Verify the amount of CO, HC. (See ENGINE TUNE-UP [ZJ, ZY, Z6].)
•  Engine-driven accessories operation
Note
•  If the engine is overhauled and installed to the vehicle, perform the road test and verify that there is no abnormality.
