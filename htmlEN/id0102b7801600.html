DTC P0011

CMP Timing over-advanced
DETECTION CONDITION
•  Actual valve timing is over-advanced by 17 ° (when the following conditions are met) from the target valve timing when the OCV is controlled at the maximum valve timing retard condition.
MONITORING CONDITION
―  Engine speed is below 4,000 rpm.
―  Engine coolant temperature is 70—110 °C {158—230 °F}.
Diagnostic support note
•  This is a continuous monitor (CCM).
•  MIL illuminates if the PCM detects the above malfunction condition during the first drive cycle.
•  FREEZE FRAME DATA is available.
•  DTCs are stored in the PCM memory.
POSSIBLE CAUSE
•  OCV malfunction
•  Spool valve in the OCV is stuck in the advance position.
•  Variable valve timing actuator is stuck in the advance position.
•  Loose timing chain or improper valve timing due to timing chain slippage
•  PCM malfunction
