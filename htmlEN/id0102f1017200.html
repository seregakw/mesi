DTC P0549

Exhaust gas temperature sensor (middle) circuit high input
DETECTION CONDITION
•  The PCM monitors the input signal from the exhaust gas temperature sensor (middle). If the voltage from the exhaust gas temperature sensor (middle) is more than 4.96 V, the PCM determines that there is a malfunction in the exhaust gas temperature sensor (middle) circuit.
Diagnostic support note
•  The MIL illuminates if the PCM detects the above malfunction condition in the first drive cycle.
•  FREEZE FRAME DATA is available.
•  The DTC is stored in the PCM memory.
POSSIBLE CAUSE
•  Exhaust gas temperature sensor (middle) malfunction
•  Connector or terminal malfunction
•  Open circuit in wiring harness between exhaust gas temperature sensor (middle) terminal A and PCM terminal 57
•  Short to power supply in wiring harness between exhaust gas temperature sensor (middle) terminal A and PCM terminal 57
•  Open circuit in wiring harness between exhaust gas temperature sensor (middle) terminal B and PCM terminal 61
•  PCM malfunction
