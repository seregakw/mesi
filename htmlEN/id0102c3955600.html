DTC P0652

EGR throttle valve position sensor circuit or air bypass valve position sensor circuit or CMP sensor circuit or CKP sensor circuit low input
DETECTION CONDITION
•  The PCM monitors the input signal from EGR throttle valve position sensor or air bypass valve position sensor or CMP sensor or CKP sensor. If the voltage from EGR throttle valve position sensor or air bypass valve position sensor or CMP sensor or CKP sensor is less than 0.25 V, the PCM determines that there is a malfunction in the EGR throttle valve position sensor circuit or air bypass valve position sensor circuit or CMP sensor circuit or CKP sensor circuit.
POSSIBLE CAUSE
•  EGR throttle valve position sensor malfunction
•  Air bypass valve position sensor malfunction
•  CMP sensor malfunction
•  CKP sensor malfunction
•  Connector or terminal malfunction
•  Short to ground in wiring harness between EGR throttle valve position sensor terminal F and PCM terminal 206
•  Short to ground in wiring harness between air bypass valve position sensor terminal F and PCM terminal 206
•  Short to ground in wiring harness between CMP sensor terminal C and PCM terminal 206
•  Short to ground in wiring harness between CKP sensor terminal C and PCM terminal 218
•  PCM malfunction
