DTC P0335

Crankshaft position (CKP) sensor circuit malfunction
DETECTION CONDITION
•  The PCM monitors the input voltage from the CKP sensor signal. If the voltages at PCM deviates from the threshold, the PCM determines that the there is a CKP sensor circuit problem.
POSSIBLE CAUSE
•  CKP sensor malfunction
•  Connector or terminal malfunction
•  Short to power supply between CKP sensor terminal C and PCM terminal 218
•  Short to power supply between CKP sensor terminal B and PCM terminal 238
•  Short to power supply between CKP sensor terminal A and PCM terminal 239
•  Short to ground between CKP sensor terminal C and PCM terminal 218
•  Short to ground between CKP sensor terminal B and PCM terminal 238
•  Open circuit between CKP sensor terminal C and PCM terminal 218
•  Open circuit between CKP sensor terminal B and PCM terminal 238
•  Open circuit between CKP sensor terminal A and PCM terminal 239
•  CKP sensor pulse wheel malfunction
•  PCM malfunction

•  Turn the engine switch off.

―  CKP sensor terminal B and body ground

•  Is the voltage B+?

Repair or replace the wiring harness, then go to Step 10.
No

Yes

•  Disconnect the PCM connector.
